<p><p>Radio Frequency (RF) relays come in two main types: DC Type and AC Type. DC Type relays are mainly used for low frequency applications, such as digital circuits, due to their ability to handle direct current (DC) signals. On the other hand, AC Type relays are designed for high frequency applications, such as radio frequency signals, as they can effectively switch alternating current (AC) signals. Both types of relays play a crucial role in maintaining signal integrity and managing power distribution in various electronic systems.</p></p>
