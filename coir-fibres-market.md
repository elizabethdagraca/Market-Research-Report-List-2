<p><p>Coir fibres can be categorized into two main types: brown fibre and white fibre. Brown fibre is derived from mature coconuts and is known for its strength and durability, making it ideal for products like mats, ropes, and brushes. On the other hand, white fibre is sourced from young coconuts and is softer and finer in texture, commonly used for products like upholstery padding and mattress filling. Both types cater to different market needs and applications within the coir fibre industry.</p></p>
