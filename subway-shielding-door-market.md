<p><p>Subway shielding doors come in two main types: fully enclosed and semi-closed. Fully enclosed doors provide complete separation between the platform and the tracks, offering maximum safety for passengers. Semi-closed doors have openings at the top and bottom, allowing for airflow while still providing a barrier against falls or accidents. Both types of doors are used in subway stations to enhance passenger safety and prevent unauthorized access to the tracks, ensuring a smooth and secure commuting experience for the public.</p></p>
