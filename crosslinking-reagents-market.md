<p><p>Crosslinking reagents are used in various industries to create covalent bonds between molecules, enhancing the stability and properties of materials. Some common types include amino-based reagents, isocyanates, aziridines, carbodiimides, and others. Amino-based reagents work by reacting with amino groups in molecules, while isocyanates target hydroxyl groups. Aziridines introduce three-membered rings for crosslinking, carbodiimides facilitate bonds between carboxyl groups, and other types such as epoxides and aldehydes offer unique crosslinking mechanisms for specific applications in industries like pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and materials science.</p></p>
