<p><p>Clinical laboratory robots come in two main types: Fully automatic robots and semi-automatic robots. Fully automatic robots are capable of performing tasks without human intervention, such as sample sorting, testing, and analysis. These robots are highly efficient and can handle a large volume of samples quickly and accurately. On the other hand, semi-automatic robots require some level of human supervision and intervention, mainly for sample preparation and loading. While semi-automatic robots are not as fully autonomous as their counterparts, they still offer significant benefits in terms of efficiency and accuracy in the clinical laboratory setting.</p></p>
