<p><p>Radial tires are the most commonly used type in the car replacement tire market. They are designed with layers of fabric cords arranged at right angles to the centerline of the tire, providing better stability, traction, and fuel efficiency. On the other hand, bias tires have fabric cords crisscrossed at a 30-45 degree angle to the centerline, offering a smoother ride but less durability and fuel efficiency compared to radial tires. Both types cater to different needs and preferences in the car replacement tire market.</p></p>
