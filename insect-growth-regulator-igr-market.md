<p><p>Insect Growth Regulators (IGRs) are a type of insecticide that disrupt the growth and development of insects, leading to their eventual death. There are three main types of IGRs on the market: Chitin Synthesis Inhibitors, which interfere with the production of chitin in insect exoskeletons; Juvenile Hormone Analogs & Mimics, which mimic the effects of juvenile hormones and prevent insects from maturing; and Anti-juvenile Hormone Agents, which block the action of juvenile hormones in insects, preventing them from reaching adulthood.</p></p>
