<p><p>Fire extinguishers are classified into different types based on the type of extinguishing agent they contain. The most common types include dry chemical, foam, carbon dioxide, and others. Dry chemical extinguishers are effective against class A, B, and C fires, while foam extinguishers are used for A and B class fires. Carbon dioxide extinguishers are suitable for class B and C fires, as they displace oxygen to smother the fire. Other types of fire extinguishers include water mist, clean agent, and wet chemical, each with unique properties for specific types of fires.</p></p>
